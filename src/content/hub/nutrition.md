## Why nutrition matters for swimmers

A hard 90-minute session uses a lot of energy, and growing swimmers need to cover training and growth at the same time. Under-fuel and you'll fade in the last third of sessions, pick up more colds, and stall in training. Swim England's advice for young swimmers is straightforward: don't skip meals, snack through the day, and fuel every session.

At this level, it's mostly normal food in sensible amounts at sensible times.

## The everyday plate

- **Carbohydrate is the base.** Pasta, rice, potatoes, bread, oats, fruit. This is the fuel swimming runs on. On training days, roughly half the plate should be carbohydrate.
- **Protein at every meal.** Eggs, chicken, fish, beans, yoghurt, milk. Protein repairs muscle after training and supports growth. Spread it across the day rather than one big hit.
- **Colour on the plate.** Fruit and vegetables carry the vitamins and minerals that keep the immune system working through winter training.
- **Don't fear fat.** Nuts, oily fish, avocado and dairy support hormones and long-term energy in growing athletes.

## Around training

**Before (2–3 hours out):** a normal carb-based meal: pasta, jacket potato, rice dish. If the session is straight after school, a substantial snack at ~4pm works: bagel with peanut butter, banana and yoghurt, cereal and milk.

**During:** for our sessions, water is enough. A squash-filled bottle is fine for longer, harder sessions.

**After training:** eat something with carbs and protein within 30–60 minutes. Late evening sessions make this awkward, so plan ahead:

- Flavoured milk or a milkshake
- Yoghurt and a banana
- A wrap or sandwich prepared before the session
- Cereal with milk when you get home

## Hydration

Swimmers sweat. You just can't see it in the pool. Arrive at every session having drunk regularly through the day, bring a full bottle to poolside, and drink between sets. Pale-straw urine colour is the simple check. In hot pools or hard sessions, drained bottles are a good sign, not a problem.

## Race day

At a gala you might race four times across five hours. The routine shifts slightly:

- **The day before:** regular meals and snacks rich in carbohydrate to top up glycogen stores, nothing heavy late in the evening, and only familiar foods. Race weekend is not the moment for a new curry.
- **Breakfast:** familiar and carb-based: porridge, toast and jam, cereal. Never race on empty, and never trial a new breakfast on race day.
- **Between races, go by time to your next swim** (Swim England's rule of thumb): under 30 minutes, stick to fluids or a few grapes or bites of banana; 30–60 minutes, a light snack like a cereal bar, rice cake or half a sandwich; 1–2 hours, something more substantial like a full sandwich or pasta salad; over 2 hours, a proper balanced meal.
- **After the last race:** proper recovery snack or meal as soon as possible. It's still training load, and there may be another day of racing tomorrow.

**Pack in the gala bag:** two full bottles (water + squash), bananas, malt loaf or cereal bars, small sandwiches, and a back-up snack for delays. Sports halls have vending machines and queues; a packed food bag beats both.
